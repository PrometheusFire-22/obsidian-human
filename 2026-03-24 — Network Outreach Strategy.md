# Network Outreach Strategy — Tangible Markets & Broader Reconnection

**Context:** In discussions with Tangible Markets (London-based PE secondary brokerage/advisory) for a Sales Operations Analyst role (remote). Using this as a forcing function to reconnect with industry relationships, get a read on market comp, and implicitly surface broader opportunities.

**Framing principle:** This is not a job search call. It's a reconnection call where you're asking for their read on the market. The Tangible Markets situation is colour, not the ask.

> **Constraint (confidential):** An ongoing legal matter — known to most of these contacts from when it occurred — is expected to resolve in ~2 months. Until then, broad professional re-engagement carries reputational risk that outweighs the upside. Most outreach should wait until post-resolution, when the hire story is clean. Exceptions below.

---

## Active Now vs. Wait for Resolution

| Contact | Reach Out Now? | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Contact 1 (close friend) | **Yes — this weekend** | Personal, internal, knows everything |
| Contact 2 (former coworker, competitor firm) | **Yes — this weekend** | Time-bounded, specific ask, he knows the situation |

| Contact 6 (MD at PE fund) | Wait | Weakest relationship; no time pressure |

---

## Priority Tier 1 — Highest Value, Move First

### Contact 1: Close Friend / Former Desk Neighbour
**Relationship:** Strongest personal bond, deep industry seniority, stuck by you through hard times.
**Value:** Best read on your actual market value, most candid feedback, most likely to refer or advocate without agenda.
**Angle:** This is the one person you can be fully honest with. Don't frame this as a market research call — just tell him what's happening, including the Tangible Markets situation and your ambivalence about it. Ask him directly: *"What would you do? And do you know anyone I should be talking to?"*
**Ask:** His honest read on whether the role is worth it, what the firm's reputation is in the market, and whether anyone in his network is hiring for something more front-office adjacent.
**Timing:** Call or text this weekend. Don't wait.

---

### Contact 2: Former Coworker Now at Tangible Markets Equivalent
**Relationship:** Worked deals together, knows your situation, early employee at a direct competitor to Tangible Markets.
**Value:** He can tell you exactly what Tangible Markets is operationally, what the culture is likely like, whether the role is a dead end or a door, and what comp looks like at his shop for a comparable role.
**Angle:** "I've been approached by Tangible Markets for an ops role and before I respond I wanted to get your read — you're doing essentially the same thing over there. What's your sense of them and what should I know?"
**Ask:** (1) What's comp like at his shop for a similar role — ops-adjacent with domain expertise? (2) Is his firm hiring or growing? (3) What's he hearing about Tangible Markets' trajectory?
**Timing:** This weekend or Monday morning before you respond to Tangible Markets.
**Note:** This is also the most natural implicit plug. If his firm is hiring, you've just had the conversation.

---

## Priority Tier 2 — High Upside, Handle with Care

### Contact 3: Pioneer Founder (Former Employer)
**Relationship:** Pioneer in the field, runs the brokerage + news platform + data provider + ATS. Wildly successful. You worked for him.
**Value:** The highest-signal reference point for what your profile is worth and where the industry is going. If he has something for you, it would be the best possible outcome.
**Angle:** Don't lead with job search. Lead with genuine curiosity about what he's building — the ATS and data platform angle is genuinely interesting and you can ask intelligently about it. Let him bring up your situation or create a natural opening.
*"I've been heads-down building AI workflows for the past couple of years and I'm starting to think about where that intersects with what's happening in private markets infrastructure. I'd love to catch up — I've been following what you've been building."*
**Ask:** What he's seeing in the market. Where he thinks the industry is in 5 years. If it comes up naturally — whether there's a place for someone with your combined background.
**Timing:** Send a note early next week. This one merits a slightly more considered outreach message.
**Caution:** Don't mention Tangible Markets unless he asks. He may have opinions about them and you don't want to anchor yourself there before he's thought about you independently.

---

### Contact 4: Private Stock Trading Platform (Vancouver, $600M+ valuation)
**Sub-contacts ranked by relationship:**
1. Co-founder / #2 executive — closest relationship, most likely to respond
2. Senior broker — good relationship, relevant to your deal-making background
3. Second senior broker — weaker but positive
4. Founder/CEO — originally hired you at Pioneer's firm; highest upside, lowest probability of response given their growth stage

**Value:** High-growth, well-capitalized, doing something genuinely adjacent to your background (private markets liquidity, VC-backed direct shares). If they're scaling fast they may need senior people who know the space.
**Angle:** Lead with genuine interest in what they've built. The $600M valuation and growth story is a natural conversation starter. *"I've been watching what you've been building — the growth has been remarkable. I'd love to catch up and hear where you're taking it."*
**Ask:** What the build-out looks like. Whether they're hiring in roles that combine domain expertise and ops/technology. Whether your AI workflow background is interesting to them.
**Timing:** Reach out to #1 (co-founder) first, this week. Don't blast all four at once.
**Note:** They're Vancouver-based and moving fast — response time may be slow. Don't let this block you.

---

## Priority Tier 3 — Reconnection + Market Intelligence

### Contact Group 5: Pioneer Spinout (5 people, private capital advisory)
**Relationship:** Varying strength, all positive. They do LP/GP/VC-backed direct share advisory — directly in your wheelhouse.
**Value:** Market intelligence, comp data, and implicit opportunity. If they spun out and are growing, they're likely hiring or will be.
**Angle:** This is the group where you can be most direct about "I'm thinking about what's next and wanted to reconnect with people doing interesting work in the space."
**Sequencing:** Start with the strongest relationship. Don't reach out to all five at once — it reads as a mass campaign. Stagger over 1–2 weeks.
**Ask:** What they're working on, how the business has grown, what skills they find hardest to hire for. Let the conversation do the work.

---

### Contact 6: Former Relationship — Now MD / #2 at PE Fund
**Relationship:** Historical, positive but not close. Works at actual PE fund (not advisory/brokerage).
**Value:** Different angle — PE fund-side perspective on what they look for, what ops/deal roles look like from the buy side, and potentially a reference or warm intro elsewhere.
**Angle:** Lower stakes reconnection. *"I've been out of the industry for a few years and am reconnecting with people I respect. I'd love to hear what you've been up to."*
**Ask:** His read on the market from the fund side. Less likely to have a direct opportunity for you but good for triangulating market intelligence and reactivating the relationship for the future.
**Timing:** Later in the sequence — Week 2.

## Message Templates

### Short Text / LinkedIn DM (for close contacts)
> "Hey [name] — it's been too long. I'm starting to re-engage with the industry after a few years away and you're one of the first people I wanted to reconnect with. Would love to catch up for 20 minutes if you have time this week or next. How are things?"

### Slightly More Formal (for Pioneer, CEO-level)
> "Hi [name] — I hope you're well. I've been following what you've been building at [firm] and it's been impressive. I've spent the last couple of years deep in AI workflow development and I'm starting to think about how that intersects with private markets infrastructure. I'd love to reconnect and hear your perspective on where things are heading. Would you have 20 minutes sometime in the next few weeks?"

---

## What NOT to Do

- Don't mention you're unemployed or frame this as a job search in any opening message
- Don't blast multiple contacts at the same firm simultaneously
- Don't bring up Tangible Markets with Contact 3 (Pioneer) unless he asks
- Don't reach out to the CEO/Founder of the Vancouver platform first — go through the co-founder
- Don't send a long message. Short, warm, specific = higher response rate

---

## Tangible Markets Response Timing

Hold until **Monday mid-morning GMT**. By then you'll ideally have spoken to Contact 1 and/or Contact 2 and will have a better sense of whether this role is worth anchoring high or whether you have better options emerging. Don't let their timeline rush your decision.
