## 1. Market ranges (London PE / secondary advisory)

For a Sales Operations / Revenue Operations Analyst sitting in a London-based PE or secondary advisory context, 2025–2026 market bands look roughly like:

- **Junior (true 1–3 years, not you)**
    
    - Base: ~£35k–£45kpayscale+1
        
    - Bonus: ~10–25% of base in ops at advisory/brokerage firms (so ~£3.5k–£11k)payscale+1
        
    - Total comp: ~£38k–£56kpayscale+1
        
- **Mid-level / experienced analyst (5–9 years overall, strong ownership, closer to your profile even if title is “analyst”)**
    
    - Base: ~£45k–£65k in general sales ops, but in private markets / PE-flavoured shops it often creeps into the £60k–£80k band when the role is more critical and hybrid ops / GTM / analytics.payscale+3
        
    - Bonus: ~15–35% is a reasonable expectation for a revenue-facing ops role in a profitable advisory business; more if they’re aggressive on variable comp.carnegieconsulting+1
        
    - Total comp: ~£70k–£100k is a realistic mid-career expectation, with top-of-band roles edging to ~£110k when closer to “business ops / commercial ops” than pure admin.payscale+2
        

If they truly position it more like “investment / PE operations analyst” rather than generic sales ops, some London roles advertise bases up to ~£85k for experienced ops analysts in private assets.[cornwalliselt](https://www.cornwalliselt.com/job/operations-analyst-1)

## 2. How to translate to CAD and remote-Canada reality

If they pay you in **GBP at London bands** and you stay fully remote in Canada, you’re effectively getting London comp with a Canadian cost base. Many UK employers now either:

- Put remote employees on UK bands and pay in GBP, or
    
- Localize pay to CAD with a discount of roughly 10–25% vs the UK benchmark, citing local market differences.reddit+1
    

Given that:

- A **“strong” mid-level analyst target ask** (for you, with senior background) if paid on UK rates in GBP could reasonably be:
    
    - Base: £65k–£75k
        
    - Bonus target: 20–30%
        
    - Total: ~£80k–£95k
        
- If they insist on **CAD-localized comp at a discount**, the same economic value roughly translates into:
    
    - Base: CAD ~115k–135k (back-of-envelope from those GBP ranges using 2025–2026 FX bands)
        
    - Total comp: CAD ~135k–165k
        

You do not want to anchor closer to the £35k–£45k “average UK sales ops analyst” numbers that generic salary sites surface.jooble+2

## 3. Currency and structure (what you should ask them)

Your layering is exactly right; I’d explicitly bake these checks into the prompt you give the model or your own prep notes:

- “For UK firms hiring fully remote in Canada, is this role typically benchmarked to UK London salary bands and paid in GBP, or localized to CAD with a discount on UK bands? Please answer explicitly which practice is more common in 2024–2026 for similar roles.”linkedin+1
    
- “When ranges are quoted, treat me as a mid-level / senior operator sitting in an ‘analyst’ title, not as an entry-level analyst. Use experience bands (5–10 years) where possible, and ignore 0–3Y data.”payscale+1
    

In negotiation, your first factual question to the recruiter should be:

- “Is this role on the London band paid in GBP, or is compensation localized to CAD? If localized, what discount vs. your London benchmark do you apply?”
    

## 4. Seniority and your position

Based on market data, “mid-career” sales ops analysts in London (5–9 years experience) sit around £45k–£50k total comp in generic SaaS / commercial environments. PE / private markets and finance-adjacent roles can run hotter, especially when they blend ops, analytics, and revenue enablement, pushing into that £60k–£80k base band.carnegieconsulting+4

Given your track record, you are functionally sitting above this median:

- Don’t let them map you to the £30k–£40k “early career analyst” bucket that salary sites show.jooble+2
    
- Frame yourself as “mid/senior commercial operator working at an analyst title level” and quote bands accordingly.
    

## 5. AI workflow premium (how to actually monetize it)

Right now, generic salary data does not explicitly price “agentic AI workflow experience” yet, but roles that list Salesforce, analytics, or automation skills earn a clear premium vs baseline. For example, Sales Ops Analysts with Salesforce skills in London earn a higher average (~£36.5k base vs ~£34k), implying ~5–10% uplift for specialized tooling skills.payscale+1

Your agentic AI workflows are a higher-order equivalent of that skill premium. Practically, that means:

- Expect **0–10%** explicit premium inside the band at offer time (they rarely rewrite the band around one candidate).payscale+1
    
- Use AI capabilities to justify:
    
    - Top-of-band base (e.g., closer to £75k than £60k), and
        
    - Ambitious variable comp structure (e.g., upside if your automations drive measurable deal throughput or reduced headcount).
        

So: don’t bake a huge “AI multiplier” into your initial range, but absolutely use it as the narrative to pull you to the **top end of whatever band** they admit to.
